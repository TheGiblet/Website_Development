About Me

Hi, I'm James Gilbert, a robotics enthusiast with a passion for creating innovative and intelligent machines. My path to robotics has been a bit unconventional, but it's been driven by a lifelong fascination with how things work and a desire to prove that I can achieve anything I set my mind to.

Growing up with dyslexia and ADHD, I was often told I was "stupid" and wouldn't amount to much. My parents even encouraged me to become a carpenter, believing it was a "job for life." However, developing gout, arthritis, tendonitis, and tennis elbow forced me to reconsider that path.

Undeterred, I embraced new challenges and pursued various trades, including locksmithing, water hygiene engineering, and watch repair. I even kitted out ambulances and worked for e3d, where I progressed from production to QC, sales, and customer service. I also had the opportunity to develop a product for them, which was a rewarding experience.

My passion for technology led me to teach myself 3D modeling, animation, rendering, and texturing. I've built 3D printers as large as 2 meters squared and even ran a 3D printing business with 10 printers at one point.

Most recently, I've been diving into the world of Python coding, creating art, games, a Pi GPIO planner, chatbots, Tamagotchi games, and, of course, robotics projects. I believe that robots have the potential to revolutionize various industries and improve our lives in countless ways.

My journey has taught me the importance of perseverance, adaptability, and continuous learning. I'm excited to continue exploring the world of robotics and contribute to its exciting future.

Feel free to contact me if you'd like to connect or collaborate!

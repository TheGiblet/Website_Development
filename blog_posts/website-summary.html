Today's Website Building Summary (Or, How We Avoided a Fate Worse Than Death)

December 9, 2024

I say, what a spiffing day it's been! We've managed to cobble together a website that doesn't resemble Baldrick's attempts at soup (i.e., a bucket of mud with a dead rat floating in it). Indeed, I dare say it's almost... presentable. Now, let's review the fruits of our labor, shall we?

1. HTML Structure (The Foundation of Our Digital Empire)

  • Erected the basic HTML framework, much like constructing a medieval siege engine (but with fewer splinters and considerably less risk of being catapulted into a moat).
  • Added a navigation bar, allowing visitors to traverse the site with the ease of a Regency dandy strolling through Hyde Park.
  • Crafted separate pages for projects, about me, blog, contact, and products, ensuring that our digital domain is as well-organized as a Blackadder insult.
  • Organized projects into categories, because even Baldrick knows that one cannot simply throw turnips and sheep into a single pile and call it a cunning plan.
  • Adorned each page with a header and footer, much like a Victorian gentleman sporting a top hat and spats (but with considerably less risk of contracting gout).

2. CSS Styling (Or, How We Made the Website Less Hideous Than a Nun's Underwear)

  • Conjured a `style.css` file, imbuing the website with visual flair that would make even the most discerning aesthete (or, indeed, Lord Flashheart) raise an eyebrow in approval.
  • Added styles for general elements, ensuring that the text doesn't resemble a ransom note penned by a drunken pirate.
  • Styled the navigation bar, header, and other sections with the precision of a master swordsman (but with considerably less blood and gore).
  • Added styles for the accordion sections, because even the most complex contraptions should unfold with the grace of a Regency courtesan's fan.
  • Refined the CSS, transforming the website from a visual abomination to something almost... tolerable.
  • Made the website responsive, ensuring that it adapts to different screen sizes with the versatility of Baldrick's cunning plans (though with a significantly higher success rate).

3. JavaScript Functionality (Or, How We Brought the Website to Life Without the Use of Necromancy)

  • Infused the accordion sections with JavaScript, allowing them to expand and collapse with the fluidity of a Shakespearean monologue (but with considerably less iambic pentameter).
  • Integrated the lightGallery library, enabling visitors to admire images in a lightbox, much like a Victorian gentleman perusing his collection of exotic butterflies (but with considerably less risk of contracting malaria).

4. Content Creation (Or, How We Filled the Website With Words That Are Not Utterly Inane)

  • Crafted compelling content for the projects page, detailing my robotic creations with the eloquence of a seasoned orator (but with considerably less hot air).
  • Penned an "About Me" page that is both informative and mildly amusing, much like a Blackadder episode (but with a slightly higher survival rate for the main characters).
  • Added a "Contact" page, because even hermits occasionally need to communicate with the outside world (though preferably not through the medium of carrier pigeons).
  • Wrote this very blog post, a testament to my ability to string words together in a semi-coherent fashion (unlike Baldrick, whose sentences often resemble a drunken spider attempting to tap dance).

5. Image Handling (Or, How We Tamed the Wild Beasts of the Digital Image Jungle)

  • Adorned the website with Python-generated images, proving that even a simpleton like Baldrick can be useful (with the right amount of supervision and a very large cattle prod).
  • Implemented watermarks on said images, because even in the digital age, one must protect one's intellectual property from plagiarizing scoundrels (and art thieves disguised as nuns).
  • Scaled the images to fit their containers, ensuring that they don't resemble a hippopotamus attempting to squeeze into a tutu.
  • Made the homepage header image display at its original size, because, unlike Lord Blackadder, I believe in making a grand entrance.

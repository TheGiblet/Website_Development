Building My Robotics Website: A Journey of Creation and Learning

December 9, 2024

Hello, fellow robotics enthusiasts! I'm excited to share my journey of creating this website, a platform to showcase my passion for robotics and the projects I've been working on. As someone who thrives on bringing ideas to life through innovation and technology, I felt it was time to establish a dedicated space to document and share my creations.

The decision to build this website stemmed from a desire to not only showcase my completed projects but also to track the progress of those still in development. I wanted a place where I could articulate the challenges I faced, the solutions I devised, and the valuable lessons learned along the way. Moreover, I envisioned this website as a hub for connecting with other robotics enthusiasts, sparking collaborations, and inspiring those who share a similar passion.

Bringing the Vision to Life

The process of building this website has been an enlightening experience. I delved into the world of web development, familiarizing myself with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. With each line of code, I witnessed my vision taking shape, transforming from an abstract idea into a tangible online presence.

One of the most rewarding aspects was crafting the individual pages. The "Projects" page, for instance, allowed me to organize and present my robotics endeavors in a structured manner. Categorizing them into "Robotics Projects," "Python Coding Projects," "3D Modeling Projects," and "Future Projects" provided a clear framework for visitors to explore my work.

The Power of Python-Generated Images

A unique feature of this website is the inclusion of Python-generated images. Leveraging Python's versatility and my coding skills, I was able to create visually captivating headers and footers that add a distinct touch to the website's aesthetic. These images not only enhance the visual appeal but also serve as a testament to my programming capabilities.

The process of generating these images involved experimenting with various Python libraries and techniques. I was particularly fascinated by the ability to manipulate and transform images programmatically, resulting in unique and creative visuals. The "watermark" effect subtly embedded in these images is a testament to the power and flexibility of Python.

Reflections and Looking Ahead

Building this website has been a journey of learning and discovery. I've gained a deeper appreciation for the intricacies of web development and the power of visual presentation. The process has reinforced the importance of meticulous planning, attention to detail, and a willingness to adapt and learn.

Looking ahead, I'm excited to continue expanding this website. I plan to add more detailed project descriptions, insightful blog posts about my robotics experiences, and potentially even tutorials to guide aspiring roboticists. I envision this website evolving into a dynamic platform that fosters a community of like-minded individuals passionate about the world of robotics.

Thank you for joining me on this journey. I invite you to explore the website, delve into my projects, and connect with me if you'd like to collaborate or simply share your thoughts on robotics. Let's continue to push the boundaries of innovation and technology together!

Gismo's New Look: A Custom 3D-Printed Chassis!

December 11, 2024

Exciting news! Gismo is getting a brand new, custom-designed body. After successfully testing the prototype on a breadboard, I decided it was time to give Gismo a more permanent and robust home.

I found a great starting point on Thingiverse: a tracked robot chassis called the Cosmarzs 2 (https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:4910801). The tracked design will give Gismo better traction and stability, allowing it to navigate a wider variety of terrains.

3D Printing and Modifications

3D printing the Cosmarzs 2 was a fun experience, but I quickly realized that some modifications were needed to perfectly accommodate Gismo's components. The Raspberry Pi, ESP32, sensors, and battery all have specific size and placement requirements.

So, I've been putting my 3D modeling skills to the test, adjusting the original design and even redesigning some parts from scratch. It's been a great learning process, and I'm happy with how the custom chassis is coming together.

A Sneak Peek

What's Next

Once I'm satisfied with the 3D-printed parts, I'll move on to the final assembly. This will involve carefully mounting all the components, wiring everything up, and making sure everything fits snugly within the chassis.

Gismo's Coming Together: A Major Component Installation Spree!

December 12, 2024

Yesterday was a marathon of component installation for Gismo! I managed to get a significant number of essential parts integrated into the chassis. Here's a rundown of the new additions:

  • Power System: The battery and charger are now securely in place, ensuring Gismo has the juice it needs to operate.
  • Sensing the World: The ultrasonic sensor is ready to help Gismo perceive its surroundings and avoid obstacles.
  • Navigating the Terrain: Floor sensors are installed, enabling Gismo to detect edges and navigate effectively.
  • Motion Control: The motor driver is hooked up, giving Gismo the power to move and groove.
  • Audio Feedback: A buzzer is integrated to provide auditory cues and alerts.
  • Visual Cues: An LED is ready to shine, providing visual feedback on Gismo's status.
  • Remote Control: The IR receiver is in place, allowing for remote control functionality.
  • Interactive Touch: A touch sensor is installed, enabling Gismo to respond to touch interactions.
  • Orientation Awareness: The MPU (most likely an MPU-6050 IMU) is integrated, providing Gismo with crucial orientation and motion data.

It's quite a sight to see all these components coming together within the custom-designed chassis!

Adapting and Overcoming

Due to space constraints within the chassis, I had to make some tough decisions and sacrifice a few components:

  • The IR reverse sensor had to be omitted to make room for other essentials.
  • The Pi camera rev 1.3 was replaced with a smaller version to save space.
  • The tilt sensor was deemed unnecessary and removed to optimize space and complexity.

These adjustments highlight the importance of adaptability and prioritization in robotics projects. Sometimes, you have to make trade-offs to achieve the best overall outcome.

Next Steps

With the majority of components installed, the next phase involves integrating the brains of the operation:

  • Raspberry Pi: The Raspberry Pi will be the central processing unit, orchestrating Gismo's actions.
  • PCA: The PCA (likely a PCA9685 servo driver) will be added to control the servo motors, enabling precise movements.

I'm eager to complete this stage and move on to the exciting part: programming Gismo's behavior and bringing it to life!
